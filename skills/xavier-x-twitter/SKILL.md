## Frameworks

- **How X decides who sees you (the scoring math)** — X scores every post by guessing how much engagement it'll get, and it weights the different actions very unevenly. A reply is worth roughly 13 to 27 times more than a like, depending on how you measure. The single most powerful signal is a reply that *you* then reply back to — that little back-and-forth conversation is worth more than a hundred likes. Profile clicks, follows, time spent reading, and bookmarks all count strongly in your favour. The flip side is brutal: a mute, block, "not interested", or report wipes out dozens of good signals in one tap. So the whole game is simple to say — spark replies and profile clicks, keep people reading, and never give anyone a reason to mute. A little edge that adds value beats cheap bait that annoys, every time.

- **The first hour decides everything (the golden hour)** — X looks at how fast a post earns engagement early, and a post fades quickly — it loses about half its reach-power within the first few hours. Those first 30-60 minutes decide whether it gets pushed wider or quietly buried. Three moves: (1) post when *your* people are actually online, not some generic "best time" — your own analytics show your real peak; (2) within the first minute, drop a strong reply on your own post (the missing context, the spicy follow-up) to kick-start momentum and give early readers a second thing to engage with; (3) if a post flops, never delete and repost it — let it go, then quote-tweet it later instead.

- **Replying your way up (the reply ladder)** — Replies only grow you when yours is the *top* reply people actually see. Aim at accounts about 2-5x your size (not 10x, where you'd drown under hundreds of other replies). Get in within the first 5-10 minutes while the post still has few replies. And make your reply strong enough to stand on its own — a counterpoint, a specific example, a sharper way of putting it. While you're in growth mode, set a daily reply count you can actually keep up. One a day should be a friendly "well, actually" that earns a quote-tweet, not just a like. **Watch out for this trap:** being pure reply-guy with no posts of your own — replies get you *seen*, but only your own posts turn that profile visit into a follow.

- **Turning reach into followers (the follow funnel)** — A viral post that doesn't get people clicking your profile is a leak, not a win. So build in a "wait, who *is* this person?" pull — a claim only an insider would make — then let your bio close the deal: proof + one specific promise + one clear next step. Reach comes first, but the follow is the real payoff. (If you're on Premium, high-impression posts also earn you a slice of ad revenue, so a big post can pay even when it doesn't convert — but it still won't snowball into growth without the follow.)

## Formats Menu (pick by job)

X isn't text-only anymore — video and images hold their own, and video autoplays right in the feed. One thing worth knowing: per post, text still narrowly wins on *engagement rate* (the share of viewers who interact), with images close behind and video a touch lower — but video usually wins on *reach* (how many people see it), and X keeps leaning into it more. So "video reaches more people" is true for getting *seen*, not always for getting *interaction* — worth testing in your own niche. Here's where I'd start:

- **Single spicy one-liner** — best for authority / B2B / niche experts. One dense, quotable claim often beats a whole thread (threads can feel try-hard from an established voice). **My default for authority.**
- **List tweet** ("7 things I'd tell my younger self:") — people read the whole thing, and it's easy to quote-tweet.
- **Before/after** and **screenshots/receipts** (DMs, dashboards, results) — proof you can't fake. It keeps people reading and earns saves and replies. **My default for reach + credibility.**
- **Native video** — strong reach in most niches; your first frame *is* the hook.
- **Thread** — 5-9 tweets, with a sharp drop-off after the first few. Keep the opening tweet tight so it doesn't get cut off behind a "Show more". **My default for leads** (thread → bio → follow).

Quick guide by goal: authority → one-liners plus the occasional thread; reach → image/video + a contrarian take; leads → a thread that funnels people to your bio.

## The Truth About Links

Putting an external link in the main tweet cuts your reach hard — figure on losing 50-90% of it. For regular (non-Premium) accounts, link posts now fall to almost no engagement at all. Here's what works instead, roughly in order: **put the link in your bio** and say "link's in my bio" or "comment WORD and I'll send it"; or drop the link *after* the post already has momentum, once X has finished scoring it (the old "link in your first reply" trick is unreliable now — assume it can get buried too, and test it). If you're on Premium, long-form **Articles** keep readers on X and skip the link penalty entirely — great when the content itself is the payoff. Premium also gives a real reach boost overall, somewhere around 2-4x versus non-Premium.

## Worked Example

> **Brief (hypothetical):** solo SaaS founder, ~800 followers, on Premium, wants authority + inbound leads. Belief to plant: "you don't need funding to win."

>
> **Seeded first reply (post within 60s):** *The whole stack was 4 tools, ~$80/mo. Comment STACK and I'll DM the list — not dropping a link here, it tanks reach.*
>
> **Hook test:** both lines show before the cutoff, line 1 sets the stakes, line 2 pays off with proof. Survives. ✓
>
> **First hour:** post at this account's real 8am ET peak; in the first 10 minutes, reply to 3 build-in-public accounts about 3x its size (each still with few replies) with a sharp counterpoint. **Re-share plan:** about 6 hours later, quote-tweet the anchor — *"This got a few 'lucky' replies. It wasn't luck, here's the boring part:"* — to put it in front of a fresh slice of the feed.
>
> **Bio rewrite (closes the leak):** *Bootstrapped [tool] to $4k MRR solo, no funding. I post the messy version. Stack + playbook → comment STACK.*

The moves most people miss: the link lives in a comment-word DM (not the post, not the reply); the screenshot does the heavy lifting on keeping people reading; the quote-tweet squeezes a second life out of the same post; and the bio catches the rush of profile clicks instead of letting them leak away.

## Edge Cases (default → override)

- **Under ~1k followers:** flip the usual advice — grow through replies, not your own posts. One anchor post plus a lot of well-aimed replying beats five posts shouted into an empty room.
- **Niche-authority / B2B:** single dense tweets often beat threads; a thread can read as try-hard from an established expert.
- **Reacting to news:** X uniquely rewards a fast, real-time take over evergreen content — there's almost no "people find it later through search" tail here (unlike LinkedIn or YouTube). So a quick, sharp take on a breaking moment beats a polished evergreen thread.
- **A thread under 7 tweets that still feels thin:** cut it down to a list tweet — a thin thread does worse than one tight single post.
