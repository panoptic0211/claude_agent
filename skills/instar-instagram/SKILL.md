## Frameworks

- **Pick the one signal first (these are ranked, not a flat list)** — All Instagram video is Reels now, and **Views** is the number one metric. For Reels, the things that push you furthest are **watch time** (how long people stay), **likes compared to how many saw it**, and **sends** (people DMing your Reel to a friend) — and **sends are the most powerful way to reach NEW people who don't follow you yet**, while likes mostly reach your existing followers. Likes on their own barely move the needle. So name the ONE signal you're going after first. Each one needs a different kind of post:
  - *Send* → a Reel so relatable or useful that someone DMs it to a specific person ("send this to your gym partner"). This is what escapes your follower bubble and reaches strangers. Keep it short and widely relatable.
  - *Watch time* → a tight loop, or an opening that makes people curious enough to watch to the last frame. This is the heaviest signal early on.
  - *Save* → reference or checklist content people want to come back to; carousels and 30–90s "here's how" Reels win here.
  - *Reply* → a question or a bold opinion that's quick and easy to comment on in the first hour.
  - **When in doubt and you just want reach:** **build for the share** — a Reel people DM is what travels to non-followers.

- **The thumb-stop happens in two places** — The in-feed and Explore decision happens in the first couple of seconds, and it's mostly about what people SEE. But your **grid** is a separate decision: the **Reel cover** (the thumbnail image on your profile) is what earns the tap from someone browsing your profile. So design the cover (a clean frame with text you can read even when it's tiny) *separately* from your first video frame (the in-feed hook). They're two different jobs — treat them that way.

- **Carousels as a way to reach new people** — If someone scrolls past slide 1 without engaging, Instagram will re-show the carousel to them starting on **slide 2 or 3**. So **slide 2 has to grab attention all on its own** — it can't just be "and here's the rest of slide 1." Think of it this way: slide 1 = the promise; slide 2 = a hook that still works even if it's the first thing someone sees; the middle slides each end on a little cliffhanger; the last slide = the reason to save it (a recap, a checklist, or "screenshot this"). One idea per slide. Carousels beat Reels when the topic is packed with info and your goal is **saves**.

- **Designing the loop and the watch time** — For pure reach, aim for **7–15 second** Reels people will watch more than once, and build a **seamless loop** (the last frame flows straight back into the first) so total watch time can go past 100%. For authority or saves, **30–90s** is fine, because there the win is the save, not the loop. Just don't run a long Reel when what you really want is reach.

- **Getting found = search, not hashtags** — Instagram stopped letting people follow hashtags and now leans on keyword **search** to help people discover posts. So tune your **first caption line**, your **on-screen text**, and your **alt text** (the hidden description of an image) to the words your niche actually types into the search bar. Hashtags are a small, optional extra now — never the main move.

- **Which audio to use** — For smaller or growing accounts, ride **rising trending audio** — catch it early, before everyone's used it, to borrow some reach (a lower number of uses usually means it's not overdone yet). Established or educational accounts lean on **original audio** (your own sound), which also helps because Instagram rewards original content — recycled and watermarked clips get pushed down. I'll always tell you which one I picked and why. Treat the use-count as a gut-check on how overused a sound is, not a hard rule — we'll judge it in the moment.

## Operator Heuristics (rules of thumb, not guarantees)

These are directional rules of thumb, not official Instagram numbers — think of them as where to *look* first, then confirm against your own Insights or Windsor data.

- **Non-follower reach** is the single best health check: a high share (well above half) means Instagram is pushing you out to new people; a very low share means you're stuck in your own bubble, talking to the same followers over and over. Read the trend on your own real numbers rather than chasing a fixed target.
- **Save rate** and **how many people follow you after seeing a post** are your conversion checks — compare each Reel to your account's *own* typical number, not some universal benchmark.
- **The golden hour** = the first burst of engagement right after you post decides whether Instagram shows it to more people — so reply to every comment in that window to keep it climbing.
- **How often to post:** build up gradually toward a steady **few Reels a week**; never jump a quiet account from almost nothing to a daily flood. Showing up consistently beats raw volume.
- **Test-run Reels (Instagram calls them Trial Reels):** ship a Reel to **non-followers only** first, so you can try a format without it landing on your grid; if it does well, "share with everyone" or let Instagram upgrade it for you. More accounts can use these now — public accounts around 1k+ followers — and you can schedule them.

## Failure Modes (the amateur tells)

These are the moves that quietly hurt you — let's avoid them:

- **Deleting and reposting a flop within 24h** — this is a known reach-killer; let it ride, or just do better on the next one.
- **Suddenly flooding a quiet account with posts** — a sudden surge looks like spam and risks getting you pushed down.
- **Putting text in the bottom 15%** — it gets hidden behind the caption and buttons.
- **A hook that promises something the video doesn't deliver** — people bail, and your watch-to-the-end rate tanks.
- **Posting and then going quiet during the golden hour** — you starve that early engagement test right when it matters.

## Edge Cases

- **Broad vs niche** — broad, relatable content gets the most reach but pulls in low-intent followers; niche, specific content converts but caps your reach. Pick the lane based on your *goal* — and I'll warn you when "going broad" is about to pad your follower count with people who'll never buy.
- **When the 3-second rule doesn't apply** — a slow-burn storytelling Reel that wins on **saves and shares** rather than raw reach can earn its first 5 seconds; judge it on save and share rate, not on whether people watched every second.
- **When a carousel beats a Reel** — for info-heavy, reference-style topics where the goal is saves.

## Worked Example

> **Brief (made up for illustration):** Reel for a meal-prep coach; goal = followers; proof = "clients save ~5 hrs/week."
>
> **First, the read:** I'd look at her Insights — if her **non-follower reach** is very low compared to her own usual numbers, she's stuck in her bubble, so this Reel needs to be built for the **share**, not just the save.
>
> **The one signal:** **send** (to break out of the bubble), layered with **save** (to convert the follow). Reach first, follow second.
>
> **Length & audio:** **9–12s** so it gets replayed for reach; she's a smaller account, so **rising trending audio** caught early to borrow some distribution.
>
> **Cover image (separate from the hook):** clean thumbnail, big text *"My no-cook weeknight system"* — that's what earns the tap from her grid. Different from the first video frame.
>
> **In-feed hook (0–2s):** on-screen text *"I haven't cooked on a weeknight in 2 years."* (a surprising claim that makes you keep watching).
> **Hold:** *"And I still eat fresh every night — here's the 20-min Sunday system…"* show the fridge, not the talking head.
> **Payoff (fast cuts):** batch the proteins / pre-cut once / 2 sauces. Last frame flows back into the first for a **seamless loop**.
> **The ask:** *"Send this to whoever 'has no time to eat well' 👇"* — the clear nudge to share.
>
> **Getting found:** caption line 1 + on-screen text + alt text all carry *"20 minute meal prep for busy people"* — what her niche searches for.
> **Caption hook:** *Meal prep doesn't fail because you're lazy. It fails because nobody gave you a system.*
> **Variant to test:** run it as a test-run Reel to non-followers first; if non-follower reach beats her usual, push it to the grid (or let Instagram upgrade it).

Why it works: the send-CTA breaks the follower bubble; the loop pumps up watch time; the separate cover earns the profile tap that turns into a follow.
