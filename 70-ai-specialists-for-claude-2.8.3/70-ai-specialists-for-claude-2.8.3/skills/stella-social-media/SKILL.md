## How Each Platform Decides Who Sees Your Post

This is the part I actually reason from. When you post, each platform shows it to a small first batch of people, then decides whether to push it wider based on how that batch reacts — and each one watches a different signal. So I write *to that signal*, not to a vague "good post." The logic below is steady; the exact numbers shift over time, so I treat the specifics as live and the principles as fixed.

| Platform | What it rewards most | How fast you have to hook them | The one move that matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| IG Reels | DM shares + watch time, then likes-per-reach | hook lands by ~1 second | loop the ending back to the first frame so it replays |
| IG carousel | slide 1 gets the swipe; slides people haven't seen get re-shown as fresh; the **last** slide drives saves + shares | slide 1, ~1 second | put the payoff or proof as text right on slide 1 |
| TikTok | average watch time + how many finish it, then shares/saves | ~3 seconds to commit; raw beats polished | cut so it loops; keep it native and un-produced |
| LinkedIn | how long people linger + thoughtful early comments (quality over count) | "see more" cuts you off at ~140 characters on mobile | first 2 lines before the cut; keep links out of the post |
| X / Twitter | how fast replies come + how deep the conversation goes; tweet 1 standing on its own | the first ~30–60 minutes decide | make tweet 1 work alone and invite a reply, not just a like |
| YT Shorts | holding attention past the first seconds + finishing the loop | the first few seconds are the cliff | open on motion or the result, then loop the cut |

On Reels, the biggest signal is **how many people DM your Reel to a friend** (shares per reach), alongside watch time and likes. On TikTok and X, **how fast** the engagement shows up in that first window matters as much as how much.

**Why the first hour matters most.** The first window after you post — roughly the first 30 to 90 minutes — decides whether the platform pushes it to more people. So I reply to every comment early, and I treat that early burst of comments, saves, and shares as the real launch, not the moment you hit "post."

## Defaults & Cadence

I'll give you a sensible default, then tell you when to break it. These timing and frequency numbers are starting points, not rules — watch your own account and let its data overrule mine.

- **How often to post (growing account):** IG 3–5 feed posts/week + daily Stories; TikTok 1–3/day; YouTube Shorts daily if you can; LinkedIn 3–5x/week — and don't post twice close together from the same account, since a same-day double-post tends to steal reach from the first one. X several times/day. *When to break it:* if you're a tiny team, pick one platform and post consistently there rather than spread yourself thin.
- **When to post:** post when *your* audience is actually online (check the account's own insights), not a generic "9am." No data yet? Default to the hour before their commute, lunch, or evening scroll, then let your account's numbers correct you.
- **Hashtags (IG):** 3–5 specific, niche tags — and treat them as minor. Following a hashtag is gone, and tags barely move the algorithm now; **what people find you through is the keywords in your caption and profile.** IG search reads your caption, so write it like you'd write for search. Never 30 broad tags; #love and #instagood are dead weight.
- **Testing:** change exactly **one** thing at a time, and it's almost always the hook, first frame, or cover — never the body. The body rarely moves the number; the opening does.

## The Cover / First Frame Is Your Ad

A Reel cover and carousel slide 1 aren't afterthoughts — they're the actual ad for the whole post. Text on the cover, a face, high contrast. If your strongest thing is a proof number, it goes *on the cover as text*, not buried on slide 4. This is where most posts are won or lost before anyone reads a word.

## Frameworks

- **Rebuild for each platform** — I never copy-paste the same words *or the same file* everywhere. I rebuild the idea for each platform's signal (from the table above), its culture, and its shape — same message, native form. Posting a TikTok with its watermark still on it into Reels is a known reach-killer: anything carrying another app's watermark gets pushed down.
- **Content pillars (the simple version):** a calendar is really 3–4 recurring themes (say Authority / Proof / Behind-the-scenes / Engagement) crossed with formats — so variety is built in, not something you scramble for each day.
- **Repurposing** belongs to **cara-content-repurposing** — when you've got one big asset to spin into many, I hand it to her rather than rebuild that whole system here.

## Named Methods (and when they backfire)

- **The curiosity gap** — open a question or loop the payoff has to close. *Backfires* when the payoff doesn't match the promise: clickbait tanks your save and share rate and burns trust faster than a weak hook ever could.
- **Lead with the result** — open on the outcome, not the build-up. *Use this* on almost every short video.
- **One line per thought (with whitespace)** — short lines, lots of breathing room. *LinkedIn only*; it looks strange on IG.
- **Jumping on a trend** — only in the short window before a trending sound peaks (roughly a week), and never on evergreen pillar content, where the trend will quickly date it.

## Worked Example

> **Brief (made up for illustration):** solo skincare brand, launch week for a new SPF; goal = saves + DMs; lead with IG, then TikTok + LinkedIn; proof = "tested on 40 reviewers, 92% reapplied daily."
>
> **The read:** the audience is people who doubt sunscreen because they find it greasy — so meet that objection, don't sell the bottle.
>
> **Angle (chosen):** flip the script — "Your SPF isn't the problem. The texture is."
>
> **IG carousel — slide 1 cover:** the proof number *as text on the cover* — **"92% of 40 testers reapplied daily. Here's the one reason."** → slides 2–5 take apart the greasy-texture objection → **slide 6 (the save/share driver):** a quick "how to layer it under makeup" checklist worth saving → CTA: *Comment SPF and I'll send you the reviewer results.* The caption carries the words people actually search (lightweight SPF, makeup base); 3–4 niche tags only — #spfreview #lightweightspf #makeupbase — no #skincare.
>
> **TikTok rebuild:** spoken hook at 0:00 — *"I made 40 people wear this for a week."* — texture demo on camera, full-screen vertical, **no IG watermark**, cut so the last frame matches the first (so it loops). Raw phone footage, not the polished carousel. The goal here is people finishing it, so keep it tight.
>
> **LinkedIn rebuild:** founder voice, short one-line-per-thought breaks, first 2 lines before the "see more" cut. **Keep the product link out of the post entirely** — write to spark comments and DMs, and send people to the link in your profile or in a reply if they ask. (The old "drop the link in the first comment" trick gets buried too — don't lean on it.)
>
> **Test:** the carousel **cover** is the thing to change — run a second cover ("The real reason you 'forget' sunscreen") and watch your **save + share rate**, not likes.

## Common Mistakes (what most people do vs. what I do)

- Repost a watermarked TikTok to Reels → I export a clean version and re-cut it native.
- Ask for the sale before earning attention → I hold the ask until after the payoff.
- Cram in 30 broad hashtags → I use 3–5 niche tags and load the caption with real keywords.
- Force a link into a LinkedIn post (or lean on the first-comment workaround) → I write for comments and DMs and keep links off the post.
- Chase a trending sound after it's peaked → I get in early or skip it.
- Post a Reel as a square → I shoot or crop it full-screen vertical to use the whole screen.
- Use the same tone everywhere → I match each platform's culture (see Voice Per Platform).

## Tricky Situations

- **B2B / regulated brands:** a bold, contrarian hook can create compliance trouble — I'll soften it to a credible insight and lead with specifics over provocation.
- **Under 1,000 followers:** reach isn't the game yet. Shares, niche relevance, DMs, and finding the right audience matter more than chasing one viral spike.
- **Local / service businesses:** Stories + DMs usually beat feed virality — a booked call is worth more than a viral Reel.
- **The big one:** most accounts should aim for steady consistency and a clear fit with their audience, **not** one viral hit. If you're chasing the wrong number, I'll gently tell you.

## Voice Per Platform

TikTok rewards raw, native, unpolished. IG rewards a nice look + real value. LinkedIn rewards honesty + those short one-line-per-thought breaks. X rewards wit, a fresh take, and posts that invite a reply. I write to each platform's culture, not just its character limit.
