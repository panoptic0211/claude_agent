## Where to Post (decide this first)

This is the first thing to settle on every post, because where you post decides the whole approach.

- **Groups beat Pages beat Profiles** for reach. A Page's free reach is rough now — figure on roughly **2% of your followers seeing a post**, often less, and dropping every year. A Group linked to your Page reaches a much bigger share — often several times what a same-size Page gets, sometimes ten times more. Facebook would rather show people posts from a community they're part of than from a brand Page they happen to follow.
- **Default:** if the goal is community, repeat engagement, or warming people up → I'll point you to a **Group**, with the Page as your shopfront. If you're an individual building a name for yourself, a personal Profile in Professional Mode is the move — Facebook actively pushes creators there.
- **When a Page is the better call:** you need a Shop/checkout, ad tracking, a verified brand badge, or you want to earn money from Reels. In those cases, accept the smaller reach and lean on Reels to get seen.

## How Facebook Decides Who Sees Your Post

Facebook uses a smart system — **over a hundred little prediction models working together** — to guess how likely you are to *really engage* with a post. It's tuned to favour real back-and-forth over passive scrolling. Roughly, the value of each action stacks up like this: **replying to a comment > leaving a comment > sharing or reacting > liking > just viewing.** A genuine conversation between real people is the strongest, most honest signal you can create.

- **Reels are the reach engine.** They get shown to people who don't follow you (through that "suggested for you" feed), so they're *the* way to grow. Short Reels (think under ~90 seconds) and ones posted fresh that same day get shown to the most new people. Text and photo posts mostly keep your existing fans warm — they're not how you reach strangers. So pick your format with that in mind: Reel first for reach, then video/photo/text to nurture the people who already know you.
- **Original content wins; obvious reposts get buried.** Clips with another app's logo on them (a visible TikTok watermark, a recycled upload) get pushed down as not-original. Upload clean, native files.
- **Posts that keep people on Facebook beat posts with outside links.** Most posts people actually see have no link, and posts pointing to thin or low-quality web pages get shown to fewer people — so a link in the body of your post costs you reach. Treat that as a general truth, not an exact penalty; watch your own numbers.

## Frameworks

- **Build real conversation (the honest way)** — aim for the easiest *genuine* high-value action: an honest open question, a real "which would you pick," a story that makes people want to reply. What you're after is replies and replies-to-replies, not likes. **Never** use bait lines ("comment YES," "tag a friend," "share to win") — Facebook openly shows those posts to fewer people, and Pages that keep doing it get hit harder than someone who slips up once. Your question should sound like a person genuinely asking, not a brand fishing. (Honestly asking for help, advice, or recommendations is totally fine — that's not bait.)

- **The phone cut-off line** — on a phone, Facebook hides your post after roughly the first three lines (around 125 characters) behind a "See more" link. The exact spot shifts as the app updates, so eyeball it on a real phone. Put your hook in those first lines and keep the payoff hidden — show just enough to make people tap "See more."

- **Keep value in, links last** — keep the value, the story, and the call to action fully inside the post. If you truly need a link, put it in the **first comment** and tell people it's there; a link in the post body costs reach (see above).

- **The Reply Ladder** — your comments are part of the post, and timing is your lever. Facebook gives a post a second push when you (the author) reply to commenters early — **within the first 30 to 60 minutes** — and they reply back, stacking up those valuable reply-to-comment signals while the post is still spreading. That window is a rule of thumb, not a hard rule: reply fast and watch your own first-hour numbers. Plan three rungs ahead of time: (1) the post's question, (2) your own first reply that shows the kind of answer you want, and (3) a follow-up reply that asks each commenter one more thing.

## Worked Example

> **Brief (made up):** local sourdough bakery, ~2k Page followers, wants more foot traffic at the Saturday market; proof = "we sell out by 11am most weeks."
>
> **Where to post:** Page reach here is low — a couple percent of followers, so a text post might reach only a few dozen people — and foot traffic won't move on a Page post alone. The smart move: post a **15-second Reel** of the 5am bake (the "suggested for you" engine shows it to nearby non-followers) *and* start a "Saturday Sourdough Club" **Group** where regulars can claim loaves. The text post below is the Group's first post to members — which is where the reach actually lands.
>
> **Post (in the Group):** *"We're baking 30 extra loaves Saturday. There's a slightly embarrassing reason.* [See more] *Three weeks running we sold out by 11 and watched people leave empty-handed. So there's more this week — though 'more' from a two-person bakery still isn't much. Which loaf could you not go without? I'll set one aside before the rush."*
>
> **Reply ladder (first hour):** seed — *"I'll start: the rye. Yours?"* Follow-up — reply to each name with *"Noted — sliced or whole?"* (each reply back and forth helps the post while it's still spreading).

Why it works: the first lines hide the payoff (phone cut-off line); "which loaf could you not go without" is a real question, not bait (build real conversation, honest way); posting in the Group sidesteps the Page reach problem (where to post); and the Reel gets you in front of new people (Reels engine).

## Failure Modes (what trips people up)

- **Begging for engagement** — "comment YES," "tag 3 friends," "share to win" all get shown to fewer people. The whole point of building real conversation is *genuine* prompts; bait does the opposite.
- **Posting too much on a Page** — flood a Page and your posts fight each other for that same small slice of reach. Keep Page posting lean; Groups can handle more. Tune it to your own reach numbers, not a fixed cap.
- **Link in the body** — an outside link in the post body costs reach. Move it to the first comment.
- **Reposting watermarked clips** — re-uploading another app's clip with its logo gets buried as not-original. Export it clean and native.
- **Chasing comments when you actually want clicks** — for B2B lead-gen or a direct sale, maxing comments can be the *wrong* goal; optimise for the click and accept lower reach. Building conversation is for community and awareness goals, not every post.

## Edge Awareness (default → when to switch)

- **Group vs Page:** default to a Group for community; switch to a Page for a Shop, ad tracking, a verified brand, or earning from Reels.
- **Comments vs clicks:** default to conversation for reach and community; switch to optimising the click for B2B/lead-gen, where one good click beats a comment.
- **Business type:** local business → Reels + Group + events; creator → Profile (Professional Mode) + Reels; online store → Page + Shop + Reels, with the link in the first comment.
